Fear and Uncertainty

Edition: Limited series

This limited edition series reflects on the past five years since the COVID-19 outbreak – an era when fear and uncertainty gripped a generation worldwide.

In today’s societies, uncertainty seems to take over. It has become increasingly difficult to distinguish right from wrong, and even the role of science and medicine is being questioned.

This diptych embodies that ideological divide—two wall sconces standing in stark contrast, reflecting a world increasingly seen in black and white. Their elongated forms evoke a near-sacral presence, nodding to the almost messianic hope once placed in vaccines.

Wind Light

Edition: 100

Wind Light is a one-of-a-kind designer lamp. The poetic luminaire is produced in a limited edition, where each example is unique.

The shape of Wind Light is inspired by the forces of nature: wind, gravity and the interaction between molecules of matter. It is the physical embodiment of an intangible, digital sensation, achieved through computer simulation and 3d printing.

In designing it, I was looking for an answer to the question: how might we leverage the characteristics of 3D printing to our advantage in a collectible design object?

LetThereBe Light

Edition: Uniqe Art Piece

With LetThereBe Light, we wanted to evoke a sense of sacrality through electronics.

The luminaire combines the functions of both candleholder and mood lighting: it uses its built-in digital camera to monitor the candle flame and electronically multiplies it.

The mesmerising play of the flame is projected in real time onto the wall, creating an elevated yet cosy atmosphere.

Co-designers: Enikő Juhász and Daniel Ferenczi

Bloomaire Kinetic Chandelier

Edition: Limited series

The large-scale luminaire was commissioned by Rossana Orlandi from Diana Alchhabi and LumoConcept. By creating an elegant motion sequence, we designed the kinetics to bring the idea to life.

As the kinetic lighting designer on the team, I was responsible for the motion concept, electronic design, motors, sensors and electronics.

Design concept: Diana Alchhabi
Principal designer: András Jánosi
Mechanical designer: Szabolcs Poller-Nagy
Kinetic designer: David Salamon

Onyx

Edition: Exclusive Commission

This multi-sensory installation was created for the Michelin-starred restaurant, blending artistry and innovation to elevate the dining ambiance.

With EJTECH, we brought the grand kinetic textile installation to life through kinetic art, leveraging digital sensors, motors, and electronics.

In partnership with LumoConcept, I designed a bespoke luminaire integrated into the structure. It subtly evokes the sense of ocean waves through animated light projected onto stainless steel reflectors.

Architect: URBA
Art Direction & Sound Design: Esteban de la Torre / EJTECH
Principal Designer: András Jánosi / LumoConcept
Kinetic Design & Digital Lighting: David Salamon
